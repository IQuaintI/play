Aleksandr Solzhenitsyn

A Voice for Gulag

Chronology

  • 1918 - Born Alexsandr Isayevich Solzhenitsyn on December 11, 1918 in Kislovodsk
  • 1936 - Solzhenitsyn studied mathematics and physiscs at the Rostov State University while also delving into philosophy, literature, and history
  • 1944 - Served as a commander of an artillery battery, saw heavy combat, and was awarded the Order of the Red Star
  • 1945 - Arrested by counter-intelligence for "derogatory comments" written in a private letter and was punished with eight-years in a labour camp for "Anti-Soviet" activities
  • 1953 - Sentenced to eternal exile after completing his original sentence where he was treated for cancer
  • 1962 - Solzhenitsyn's first novel "One Day in the Life of Ivan Denisovich" is published to enormous success
  • 1966 - The "Cancer Ward" was published based off his own experience with cancer
  • 1968 - The auto-biographical "In the First Circle" was printed which depicted the lives of those that worked in prison laboratories
  • 1970 - Awarded a Nobel Prize in Literature but recieved it in 1974 due to being expelled from the Soviet Union
  • 1971 - The KGB attempted on Solzhenitsyn's life but he survived yet was left ill
  • 1971 - A series of novels titled "The Red Wheel" that told the change from Imperial Russia to the Soviet Union. The series would not be complete until 1991
  • 1973 - "The Gulag Archipeligo" was published in France which was subsequently translated into thirty-five languages and sold over thirty million copies
  • 1974 - Deported from the Soviet Union for over the "Gulag Archipeligo" where he woould bounce around Europe until landing in the United States in 1976
  • 1990 - His Soviet citizenship was restored and he returned in 1994
  • 2002 - Solzhenitsyn's final essay "Two Hundred Years Together" was released as a historical account of the Jewish people from Imperial Russia to modern Russia. It was met with criticism for its depiction of the Jewish people
  • 2008 - Dies of heart failure - buried and honored
